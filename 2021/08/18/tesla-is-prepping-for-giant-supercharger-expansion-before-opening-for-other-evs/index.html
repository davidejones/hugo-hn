Tesla is prepping for giant Supercharger expansion before opening for other EVs

zepearl 2021-08-18 20:51:03 +0000 UTC [ - ]

Tesla making superchargers available as well to cars made by other manufacturers could become an interesting development, at least during the early days.

Having read during the past months/years that Tesla's chargers are very reliable (not buggy nor just "offline" for some weird reason - e.g. just this afternoon I wanted to charge my hybrid at a specific parking garage in Zurich/Switzerland but the whole floor where the chargers are located was closed), a lot of car owners might want to use them => Tesla owners might get angry seeing that "their" chargers are all busy by non-Tesla cars => advantage of owning a Tesla would suffer.

On the other hand Tesla might get more (stable) earnings through this and the company might play with the charging cost, e.g. increasing the charging/parking cost for non-Tesla owners or at least taking more into consideration involving the cost the charging rate of the non-Tesla-car being charged and/or the time during which the charging station was busy.

I guess that the worst/most-expensive combination would be "non-tesla" + "big empty battery" + "low charging throughput"?

powerbroker 2021-08-18 23:18:40 +0000 UTC [ - ]

Allowing non-Teslas to charge at the Tesla network is no different than Tesla opening a +1 factory (virtually, the influx of non-Teslas). Tesla, even now, has two completed factories producing Teslas, and two about to go into production in 6 months. Tesla operates the chargers at a profit (or should do so soon). Accordingly, the additional cars charging, will spur provision of additional charger sites.

As a user of the Tesla network since 2016, the availability of more charging options leads to, at least, the following network effects:

* reduced need to go 'out of your way' to find a charger;

* reduced likelihood that a car runs out of charge before reaching the next charger;

* better attention to the upkeep of the charger sites and the repair of the chargers (all chargers, not just Teslas' -- competition); and

* fewer chargers under-utilized or over-utilized -- as it becomes easier to 'right size' many discrete units, rather than just a few dozen.
