ericalexander0 2021-06-13 02:25:39 +0000 UTC [ - ]

They're not wrong, on all levels. I'm a hiring manager at C# shop, that's doing plenty of ML.

Is C# a popular language? Out of the 35 recent interviews I've done, I think I've seen 5 candidates do their code exercises in C#. I've had the: are you willing to learn C# conversation with all of them. None said no.

The problem isn't do you know it, or could you learn it. The problem, for a startup, is ramp up time. New engineers have to learn the IDE and the nuances of C#.

It's a powerful language and the IDEs are great, but there's no way around the ramp up time. Can a startup accommodate the ramp up time while scaling? We did, it's possible, but I can see why a VC was concerned.
