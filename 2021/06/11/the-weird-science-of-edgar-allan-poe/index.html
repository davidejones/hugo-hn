taylorfinley 2021-06-12 19:05:17 +0000 UTC [ - ]

I'm glad this aspect of Poe is becoming more well-known! I for one was shocked when I learned that he anticipated spacetime in Eureka, where he wrote "space and duration are one." In the same work he says "because Nothing was, therefore All Things are," which is strikingly like e.g. Russell Standish's The Theory of Nothing, a very interesting take on modern cosmology.

edit: Russell Standish's book is available free for personal use, it's one of my favorites and definitely worth reading if you're in to the idea of an information theory cosmology: https://www.hpcoders.com.au/theory-of-nothing.pdf

hirundo 2021-06-12 14:52:44 +0000 UTC [ - ]

> Poe also provoked his readers with disquisitions on the technological basis for his literary work, laying out how he would take a poem “step by step, to its completion, with the precision and rigid consequence of a mathematical problem.”

So he composed poetry like many of us write code. I wonder if poetry in general shares a form of mental athletics with programming. I'd expect a compiler for poetry to be close to impossible. Though maybe a poetry linter could be useful.

In the end code is strictly constrained by reality and poetry only by imagination, making them fundamentally different. Except maybe for oddities like Poe.
