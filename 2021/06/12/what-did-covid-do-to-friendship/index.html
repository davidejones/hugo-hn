What Did Covid Do to Friendship?

France_is_bacon 2021-06-14 07:43:53 +0000 UTC [ - ]

The question for me is: What did smartphones do for friendships? For instance, when I used to go to the gym, I started conversations with others. Now everyone has their headphones on or is looking down at their smartphones and you can't strike up a conversation to make new friends. This is the same everywhere, not just the gym - that was only one example of many.

I lost my smartphone last year and just never got a new one. Don't miss it a bit, but all of you - the smartphone companies and application developers use psychologists and psychiatrists to make their apps addicting as possible, all you zombies.

jokethrowaway 2021-06-12 10:42:46 +0000 UTC [ - ]

If you need a pandemic and being told to not see people to sort your friendships, maybe you need to re-evaluate your priorities and sort out your friendships more often.

Being in lockdown made it really hard to see my existing friends and made it really hard to form new ones.

I made zero friends in my new workplace and I don't even remember how my close friends look like in real life.

There's something about real life interaction that zoom can't reproduce.

sergiomattei 2021-06-12 06:51:22 +0000 UTC [ - ]

Being distant from everyone physically allowed me to refine my circle to those who really matter, and I'm really grateful for that.

I blocked toxic friendships, erased all social media, erased many contacts, cleaned up my inboxes, etc. I keep in touch with my closest friends and any other work I'm doing. I think it's a big sanity saver to do periodic social spring cleaning.

I feel happier and at peace.

jokethrowaway 2021-06-12 10:36:23 +0000 UTC [ - ]

Glad it worked for you but you may not like it later.

I stopped contact with all my friends years ago to save my sanity when my life become too much to manage. I didn't have toxic relationships. I slowly introduced back a few people and I now talk to 2-3 people.

It was definitely out of necessity but I don't think it was the best thing to do.

If I had enough "mental power" to tolerate talking to other people other than work and family I would have kept my relationships in place.
