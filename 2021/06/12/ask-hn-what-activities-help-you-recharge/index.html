Ask HN: What activities help you recharge?

softwaredoug 2021-06-13 03:28:06 +0000 UTC [ - ]

Rituals help a lot.

Beginning of the day I exercise and get ready like I’m going to work. End of the day I shut things down (either dev tools or the whole laptop). This latter creates friction so I don’t absentmindedly a start working later in the evening.

muzani 2021-06-13 11:01:18 +0000 UTC [ - ]

Carrying my baby around. He's heavy, but nothing beats an hour long morning walk with him.

Incremental games are nice, except the 99% of them that do microtransactions. I bought all the clarusvictoria mobile games. Kairosoft was great too but pricey. Most other games stress me out, especially the ones that have a wiki. NSFW games are an interesting genre too. They're usually full of padding to unlock some scene and can be won without a wiki. I don't really look for the scene, but those scenes are the goal for the game, and it becomes a puzzle to figure out what order to press buttons.

I find horror movies and anime strangely relaxing too. Maybe because if I'm stressed out, the adrenaline needs an outlet and horror works for that.

I like history too. Greene's books are nice, just a collection of short stories. Biographies and memoirs are great too.

Also playing music. I especially enjoy playing electric bass, which maybe isn't usually that interesting by itself, but to sit for a while and play along with albums can be very relaxing.
