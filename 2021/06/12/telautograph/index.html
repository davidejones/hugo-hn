This is one of many conflicts between Elisha Gray and Alexander Graham Bell. Bell and Gray famously fought over the telephone patent, one filing just hours before the other.

What is also not as widely known is Bell also patented the autograph telegraph fax-like device.

Read more at https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elisha_Gray :

" In April, 1875, ten months before the alleged theft of Gray's design, the U.S. Patent Office granted U.S. Patent 161,739 to Bell for a primitive fax machine, which he called the "autograph telegraph." The patent drawing includes liquid transmitters. "

I'm sure there was no love lost between these two.

gene-h 2021-06-12 20:00:50 +0000 UTC [ - ]

I am surprised that this was accomplished using primitive electric motors, relays(?), and mechanical parts. This page[0] seems to have some better descriptions of how they worked.

The device seems to operate using two relay driven escapements that seem to function similarly to today's stepper motors advancing a single step forward or backward. Step signals are generated by a set of circular contacts connected to the pen mechanism on the receiver. So when the pen is moved a degree a step signal is generated.

[0]https://www.jmcvey.net/cable/elements/telautograph1.htm

dTal 2021-06-13 09:47:34 +0000 UTC [ - ]

It is funny to me that people found this useful, as it defeats the supposed purpose of a signature. A signature is supposed to be a difficult-to-forge indication of a particular person having read and approved something - of having been physically present. But with the teleautograph, there is no guarantee - none at all - that you are signing what you think you are! You could even be signing multiple things at once without knowing. What's interesting is that clearly this wasn't a practical problem - implicit social contracts held everything together for the most part. Which means that the guarantee of a signature was never really required in the first place - social contracts would work as well for that too (and for that matter, signatures are not difficult to forge either). The whole thing is just an elaborate ritual!
